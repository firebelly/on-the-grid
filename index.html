  • 606 Trail fitness & health parks

    Humboldt Park
    Chicago IL 60622 (41.9136553,-87.7201679)

    A long abandoned and neglected stretch of elevated freight train tracks cutting east-west through Northwest Chicago, what used to only be accessible by a few muddy hillside entryways is now transformed into a bustling, paved, well-lit thoroughfare. Nearly three miles of biking, jogging, and elevated ogling opportunity thanks to a hefty $50 million grant form the federal government, meant to reduce traffic and improve air quality. For years the Bloomingdale Line was a hideaway for drinking teens, taggers, homeless folks, and the occasional adventurous jogger or cross-country skier, but it's now heralded as Chicago's answer to New York's similar project, the High Line.

  • Belly Shack food

    1912 N Western Ave
    Chicago, IL 60647 (41.9162779, -87.6876159)

    Your favorite chef’s favorite chef.

    Iron Chef & Top Chef have featured many Chicagoans, thus creating internationally known personalities. But who is it that these folks look to for inspiration or for their own lunch? Your favorite chef’s favorite chef: Bill Kim. His culinary approach is fusion, in the style of Picasso towards cubism—highly trained, expertly skilled, creativity with intent and articulation. Fifteen years in the top French and Asian restaurants across the country, Bill's training and discipline comes directly from the likes of Chef Jean Banchet and Charlie Trotter. One of three restaurants Bill owns and is executive chef of, Belly Shack is defined by metaphor of the chef's life. Born in Seoul, South Korea, Chef Bill made his Humboldt Park (predominantly Puerto Rican neighborhood) location a moment for his past to meet his present. Here he unites Korean with Puerto Rican street food and sandwiches through the highest caliber of ingredients.

    Chicago can be summed up as a mixed bag of outspoken dreamers, busting their ass to build something to call thier own. Often this melting pot finds success by bringing together familiar with exotic. Belly Shack’s Hot Dog is a prime example: all beef hot dog topped with egg noodles, a kimchi pickled green papaya salsa, served within a thick slice of brioche with togarashi spiced fries on the side.

  • C.C. Ferns coffee & tea sweets

    2806 W Augusta Blvd
    Chicago, IL 60622 (41.899364, -87.6970868537)

    Weekday or weekend, the day starts here.

    Tucked away from the California and Augusta intersection, CC Ferns is a one of those hidden gems people only dream of coming across. Weekday or weekend, my day begins here. Cortado, expresso, iced-coffee, each uses Ferns' custom roast from Metropolis Coffee Company. The roast is called “Project X” and it's needfully strong with a cocoa and earth forward flavor that foregoes acidity or bitterness. While the café opened in 2015, it physically shares backrooms as well as ownership with a dimly-lit bar that’s been open since 1937. This detail allows the liquor license to be shared across spaces, making CC Ferns a must for Sunday mornings. The adorably named Boozy Steamers is a unique offering in Chicago, as no other café serves booze. Worry not, with a name like that, no one will be upset that you got started before they answered their first email of the day.

    If you are passing through before billable time, grab a doughnut and iced coffee. But if you are nearby and not about to work or drive, order the Scotch & Honey—it’s made with Famous Grouse, almond and honey, and it will give you a buzz.

  • Tastee Freez food

    2815 W Armitage Ave
    Chicago, IL 60647 (41.9173293, -87.6980592921)

    The American dream is alive and well.

    The American dream is alive and well, although it is seasonal. Open only throughout the summer months, Tastee Freez is the neighborhood respite serving the coolest treats on the hottest days. This is not a revised contemporary interpretation of a classic with a pseudo-sexy geometric logo that emphasis hexagons and icons of everything imagineable. Here we have the pater familias—dipped cones or frozen bananas, a mix-match of ultra-vernacular lettering, ample hopscotch space near the picnic-table seating, and both dairy and ice-based treats with every topping, flavor or combination you could ever come up with at a moment's notice. The line can be long, the neighborhood can be rough… order quick. If you are looking for a truly delightable moment, order a Snow Storm (blue-razpberry), it’s not on the menu and thus communicates your expertum of this popsicle stand.
